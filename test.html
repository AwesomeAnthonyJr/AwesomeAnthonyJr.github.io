Introduction

WAKE UP.YOU'VE BEEN HERE BEFORE.YOUR MEMORY HAS BEEN STOLEN.YOU LEFT THIS MESSAGE.

Waking up in a steel cage, you find a message you left yourself. You are a magician and you must reclaim your memories. Welcome to Mage Hand. As a magician, knowledge is power. Throughout over the course of your memory-seeking journey, you will learn how to cast up to 40 different spells, and though you may be defeated, if you can remember them, the spells will work for you on another attempt. So go forth through the manor, with just one goal in mind:

RECLAIM YOUR MEMORY.

Awake Entrapped

Controls

Use the number keys to move the corresponding fingers up or down.

PAUSE

The "!" symbol signifies that you can interact by pressing the Q key. By interacting, keys can be picked up, buttons can be pressed, doors can be entered, and scrolls can be read.

By pressing the Spacebar, the current hand position is stored in the sequence and the hand is closed. The current sequence of positions is displayed above the hand. By putting the right hand-position sequences together, you can cast any spell in the game.

*Note that attempting to store with a fist will clear the sequence.

With a sequence ready, press the E key to cast it as a spell. If the sequence does not make a legitimate spell, then nothing will happen.

Use the arrow keys to move.

The Memory Castle

Discovery and knowledge are the core aspects of Mage Hand, and this is tested via memorization. However, it would be very difficult to memorize every single spell you've learned throughout your journey, so what can you do when you want to cast a spell you've already encountered, but have forgotten? Not to worry, when it is safe (when there are no enemies on screen) you may enter your memory castle from the pause menu. The memory castle is a space within the magician's mind housing all of the spells they have learned thus far, available in scroll form throughout the castle. This space may also serve as a place to test your spells as well.

THE BOOK OF MEMORIES

Use this magic book, located in the middle of the bottom of the castle to quickly access all discovered spells rather than using their scrolls.

A Map of the Memory Castle

Each floor of the memory castle holds 8 scrolls that represent memories of spells. Each memory is initially veiled in shadow, as the magician's memory has been stolen. Discovering any spell for the first time will un-veil the memory and allow you to read the scrolls in the memory castle. The top floor is reserved for the 8 legendary spells created by the high magicians.

Game Objectives

Along with your main obective in escaping the manor, there are also two major side objectives that can be completed within the game before it is up. Finding all of the keys and exploring every accesible room of the manor, and collecting the legendary spells from the high magicians.

The Keys

Each room contains within it 2 keys, each leading to a different room of the manor. You don't need all of the keys to escape, but they do unlock alternate routes that may be worth exploring.

The High Magicians

The bodies of the high magicians can be scattered across the different rooms. Each corpse contains the knowledge of a different legendary spell. If you know of some way to speak with the dead, then their knowledge may be obtained.

The Game Screen

The top header displays name of the room you are currently in, PrisoN ToweR in this case.

MAster is showing the current difficulty level.

HeAlth represents the overall health of the magician. If your health reaches 0, it's game over. The healthbar indicates how much health you have left.

Posture represents the current ability of the magician to stay fighting. If your posture reaches 0 you are stunned and take double damage. The posture bar fills up the more posture damage you take, but goes down slowly over time.

The list under stAtus: displays current status effects. Specifics of these are explained on the next page.

The smaller list of hands above the main sPELLCAstiNG hand shows the current sequence in hand signs, up to 6 signs per spell.

Prior spell: displays the name of the previously cast spell.
